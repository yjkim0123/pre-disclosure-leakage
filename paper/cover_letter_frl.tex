\opening{Dear Editor,}

I am pleased to submit the manuscript entitled \textbf{``Non-Directional Pre-Disclosure Drift Under Fair Disclosure: Evidence from Korean Earnings Announcements''} for consideration in \textit{Finance Research Letters}.

This study examines whether pre-disclosure price drift around Korean earnings announcements reflects information leakage. Using 2,221 DART filings from 64 KOSPI companies (2020--2026), we find that while statistically significant pre-disclosure drift exists, it carries no directional information about actual earnings surprises (49.0\% prediction accuracy, no better than chance). In contrast, the first post-disclosure trading day responds sharply and correctly (60.6\% accuracy, $p$<0.001). We also document that 96.5\% of filings occur after market close, with important implications for event study design.

The key contribution is distinguishing between the \textit{existence} of pre-disclosure drift and its \textit{directional informativeness}---a distinction critical for evaluating regulatory effectiveness but rarely tested in the literature.

The manuscript has not been published or submitted elsewhere. All data are publicly available through DART, and analysis code is provided via GitHub for full reproducibility.
